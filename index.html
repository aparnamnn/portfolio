Sub-prime credit card, US market · Zeta
95% automated resolution on repayment calls. 33% reduction in support team costs.
Problem

Cardholders calling in were not getting fast answers. Wait times were long, and the queries were almost always the same: due dates, minimum payments, failed transactions. Users were stuck in a support queue for something that should have taken seconds. This was driving support costs upwards of $10k a month with no visibility into what customers actually needed.

Approach
  • Built an LLM pipeline to process call transcripts and tag them by intent, to understand what users were actually calling about at scale
  • Repayment queries were the biggest pain point, accounting for nearly 42% of all calls
  • Designed an AI voice chatbot targeting repayment queries so users could get answers immediately without waiting for a human agent
  • Built in fallback logic to escalate to a human agent when needed, so users were never left without a resolution path
Outcome
  • 95% automated resolution on repayment calls, meaning most users got their answer without a handoff
  • Resolution was tracked carefully. A call counted as resolved only if the query was actually answered, not just because the bot picked up
  • 33% reduction in support team costs, with headcount reduced on queues where automation covered the need
Metrics
North Star
Automated resolution rate
Measures whether users are actually getting their repayment queries resolved without a human, which is the core user outcome this project was built around.
Guardrail
Escalation rate to human agents
Ensures automation gains do not come at the cost of users who genuinely needed a human being pushed through a frustrating bot experience first.
Near-prime credit card, UK market · Zeta
CLI targeting plan covering ~15% of MAU, designed to drive spend uplift at scale.
Problem

Cardholders on a near-prime credit card often have conservative limits that do not reflect their actual spending capacity. For users who have demonstrated responsible behaviour, a higher limit is genuinely useful. It gives them more flexibility and builds a stronger relationship with the product. The infrastructure to support CLIs existed but there was no systematic framework to identify the right users, at the right time, with the right offer.

Approach
  • Worked with credit risk to define eligibility criteria for the first cohort, anchored in cardholder behaviour rather than blanket rules
  • Designed a phased rollout to learn from early cohorts before scaling
  • Built a configurable framework with engineering, allowing A/B tests on offer size and messaging without needing a new build each time
  • Ensured instrumentation was in place from the start so spend behaviour could be measured per cohort post-CLI
Outcome
  • Initial targeting plan covered roughly 15% of MAU
  • Framework built to support ongoing monetization experiments, making CLIs a repeatable growth mechanism rather than a one-time push
  • Cohort-level instrumentation in place to measure whether limit increases actually changed spending behaviour
Metrics
North Star
Spend uplift per cardholder post-CLI
Measures whether the limit increase actually changed user behaviour, which is the real signal that the intervention was useful to the cardholder.
Guardrail
Delinquency rate
Ensures eligibility criteria are identifying the right users and not driving defaults by extending credit to cardholders who cannot manage a higher limit.

Near-prime credit card, UK market · Zeta
End-to-end onboarding designed across 5 third-party integrations, every failure state mapped and every fallback defined.
Problem

For a near-prime cardholder, applying for a credit card is already an anxious experience. When the onboarding flow broke or behaved inconsistently across integrations like KYC, identity verification, and bureau checks, users had no idea what went wrong or whether their application was still alive. That uncertainty drove drop-offs that had nothing to do with eligibility. The underlying complexity was real, but users should never have had to feel it.

Approach
  • Mapped all integration touchpoints and failure states across all five integrations, with open banking being the most complex to design around
  • Worked with design to create a unified UX that masked backend complexity from the user
  • Designed for users who might drop off mid-flow and return later, ensuring the journey could resume gracefully without losing progress
  • Worked with engineering to define fallback logic for every touchpoint, accounting for edge cases that multiplied quickly across integrations
Outcome
  • Onboarding simplified the acquisition funnel and reduced friction across all pre-launch integrations
  • Every failure state has a defined user-facing response, so no user hits a dead end without knowing what to do next
  • Acquisition funnel conversion rate is the north star metric being tracked post-launch
Metrics
North Star
Acquisition funnel conversion rate
Measures whether users who started onboarding actually completed it and got their card, which is the clearest signal that the experience was coherent and trustworthy enough to see through.
Guardrail
Drop-off rate per integration touchpoint
Ensures that if conversion dips, we can pinpoint exactly which integration is failing users rather than treating the funnel as a black box.
Media.net Software Services
92% ad targeting accuracy for a $2.8B publisher. 40+ custom segments curated.
Problem

For advertisers, contextual targeting is only as good as the segments it runs on. When a high-value publisher was onboarded onto the platform, the existing segment library was not granular enough to match the right ads to their content. Advertisers were not reaching the right readers, clicks were not happening, and the CPMs reflected that. The revenue case for the deal was at risk.

Approach
  • Worked with the data team to analyse vector clusters from the publisher's content run through the contextual engine. Vector clusters group semantically similar content together, making it possible to understand what the publisher's pages were actually about at scale
  • Used cluster outputs to identify three layers of segments: main segments covering core topics, brand segments tied to specific advertiser categories, and adjacent segments capturing related content to unlock additional demand
  • Defined which gaps in the existing library needed custom segments built and iterated on definitions based on live CTR data
Outcome
  • Achieved 92% ad targeting accuracy for the publisher
  • Strong CTR on matched inventory validated that the right ads were reaching the right readers
  • Became a proof point for onboarding future high-value publishers onto the platform
Metrics
North Star
CTR on matched inventory
Measures whether the segments are actually connecting the right ads to the right content, proven by user behaviour rather than a relevance score.
Guardrail
Segment fill rate
Ensures custom segments are broad enough to match to sufficient inventory at scale and not so narrow that they go unfilled.
